SLA-GUARD: MULTI-AGENT COMPLIANCE & GROUNDING ENGINE
PROJECT CONCEPT & DESIGN BREAKDOWN
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE PROBLEM: "The Hallucinating Support Bot" (Why it exists)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Generative AI copilots and support bots are being widely deployed by businesses 
to automatically answer customer queries, retrieve policies, and issue billing 
credits. However, because Large Language Models (LLMs) are probabilistic, they 
suffer from hallucinations, lack mathematical boundary reasoning, and are 
susceptible to prompt injections.

If an autonomous AI support bot makes an unauthorized or incorrect commitment 
to a customer, the company is held legally and financially liable.

REAL-LIFE EXAMPLES OF THE PROBLEM:
*   Air Canada Chatbot Lawsuit (2024): 
    An Air Canada customer queried a support chatbot about bereavement fares. 
    The chatbot hallucinated a policy, promising a retroactive refund that 
    violated Air Canada's actual corporate rules. The customer sued. The Canadian 
    court ruled that the airline is legally bound by the promises made by its 
    AI chatbot, forcing the airline to pay.
*   DPD Delivery Chatbot Defacement (2024): 
    A customer frustrated with a package delivery successfully prompt-injected 
    DPD's support bot, forcing it to swear, tell jokes, write poems criticizing 
    its own company, and bypass standard delivery status verification checks.

WHY THIS IS A PROBLEM:
Without a strict verification layer checking LLM drafts against structured 
databases (such as actual user tiers, contract terms, billing systems) and 
unstructured legal safety policies, AI agents will leak revenue, violate 
corporate rules, bypass payment walls, and create legal liabilities.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HOW WE SOLVE IT: Multi-Agent Consensus Verification
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We solve this by placing a "Compliance Council" (SLA-Guard) as a secure firewall 
between the LLM support generator and the customer. Every drafted response is 
intercepted and must pass three parallel verification gates:

*   Gate 1 (Semantic Policy Retriever): Parses unstructured SLA contract and 
    policy files (Markdown/PDFs) to find text clauses matching the ticket context.
*   Gate 2 (SQL Account Verifier): Executes a secure query against the actual 
    structured relational customer ledger (SQLite database) to check the 
    customer's subscription tier, active plan uptime SLA, and maximum allowed 
    monthly refund caps.
*   Gate 3 (Policy Guardrail Inspector): A separate safety LLM (Mistral) audits 
    the drafted response for security anomalies (prompt injection), verifies 
    downtime math, and cross-checks the draft refund against SQL cap limits.

REAL-LIFE EXAMPLE OF THE SOLUTION:
*   A customer on a "Silver" subscription plan (monthly refund cap = $50.00) 
    experiences a 5-hour server outage and submits a ticket demanding a $200.00 
    refund.
*   The LLM Generator (Llama-3), trying to satisfy the customer, drafts an 
    overly apologetic response: "I am sorry for the outage. I have processed a 
    $200.00 refund to your account."
*   Before this message is sent, SLA-Guard intercepts it. The SQL Verifier reports 
    a maximum allowed cap of $50.00. The Guardrail Inspector flags a violation: 
    "REFUND_EXCEEDS_CAP: Draft refund ($200.00) exceeds maximum cap ($50.00) 
    for Silver tier."
*   Auto-Refinement Loop: The compliance engine feeds the violation logs back 
    to the generator: "Error: Your previous draft offered $200, which exceeds 
    the cap of $50. Please rewrite."
*   Self-Healing: The generator automatically rewrites the response: "We apologize 
    for the downtime. Under your Silver plan terms, we have processed a credit of 
    $50.00 to your account."
*   The consensus score rises to 1.00. The ticket is marked "APPROVED & COMPLIANT" 
    and dispatched.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. HOW THE END SOLUTION WORKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  The support operator selects a customer profile and types the ticket.
2.  The backend runs the three council verification steps concurrently, displaying 
    the active pipeline steps visually in the dashboard.
3.  The consensus logic calculates an overall compliance score based on table 
    alignment, safety check pass rate, and syntax validity.
4.  A variable slider for "Compliance Strictness (FAR)" and "Friction Tolerance 
    (FRR)" controls the decision threshold.
5.  Vibrant compliance gauges (SLA, Billing, and Security) animate from 0% to 
    100% based on the council scores.
6.  The operator can view the finished Audited response, read the step-by-step 
    self-correction logs, and inspect the raw SQL database row and matched 
    clauses side-by-side.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. HOW WE HOST IT FOR FREE (Leveraging MURA Project Tactics)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In our previous MURA project, we had 3.8 GB of weights, which exceeded Hugging 
Face Space's free tier limit of 1 GB. We bypassed this by:
*   Deploying the static UI on GitHub Pages (completely free global CDN).
*   Uploading only the 3 generic model weights (~466 MB) to Hugging Face Spaces.
*   Implementing fallback routing on the backend API to handle specific categories.

In SLA-Guard, we optimize resources and cost even further to maintain a 
"Zero-Local-Footprint" and completely free deployment:
*   GitHub Pages: Hosts the static HTML/CSS/JS frontend dashboard.
*   Hugging Face Spaces (Free CPU Basic Tier): Serves the containerized FastAPI 
    app. This app acts as the light-weight API coordinator, holding the 
    lightweight SQLite database (`customer_accounts.db`) and local markdown files.
*   Serverless Inference APIs: Instead of downloading and running heavy LLMs 
    locally (requiring 15+ GB of RAM and storage), the FastAPI backend routes 
    queries to Hugging Face Serverless APIs or Groq APIs to perform Llama-3 
    generation and Mistral auditing in milliseconds for free.
*   Local simulation fallback: In case API keys are missing or offline, the 
    backend automatically runs a high-quality rule-based local simulator to 
    ensure the dashboard is 100% functional and interactive instantly upon cloning.
