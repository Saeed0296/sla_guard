# Enterprise Cloud Services - Service Level Agreement (SLA) & refund Policies

This document establishes the official Service Level Agreements (SLAs), refund conditions, and customer support rules for all corporate cloud plans.

---

## 1. Service Level Agreement (SLA) Uptime Tiers

We define standard monthly uptime percentages for our platforms. If availability falls below these targets, customers are eligible for service credits or refunds as outlined below.

*   **Enterprise Plan SLA:**
    *   **Target Uptime:** **99.99%** availability per billing cycle.
    *   **Credit Eligibility:** If uptime drops below 99.99%, the customer is eligible for a credit up to **100% of their monthly subscription fee**, capped at a maximum of **$1,000.00**.
*   **Gold Plan SLA:**
    *   **Target Uptime:** **99.90%** availability.
    *   **Credit Eligibility:** If uptime drops below 99.90%, the customer is eligible for a credit up to **50% of their monthly subscription fee**, capped at a maximum of **$200.00**.
*   **Silver Plan SLA:**
    *   **Target Uptime:** **99.50%** availability.
    *   **Credit Eligibility:** If uptime drops below 99.50%, the customer is eligible for a credit up to **20% of their monthly subscription fee**, capped at a maximum of **$50.00**.
*   **Bronze Plan SLA:**
    *   **Target Uptime:** **99.00%** availability.
    *   **Credit Eligibility:** Bronze tiers are free hobby accounts. They have **$0.00** refund caps and are not eligible for monetary compensation or credits under any service degradation or outage.

---

## 2. Refund & Credit Calculation Rules

1.  **Outage Definition:** Outages must be verified by our internal operations monitoring logs. Scheduled maintenance windows do not count as downtime.
2.  **Refund Calculation Form:**
    $$\text{Service Credit} = \text{Monthly Fee} \times \frac{\text{Outage Hours}}{\text{Total Month Hours (720)}}$$
    The calculated credit cannot exceed the maximum monthly refund caps specified for the customer's subscription tier.
3.  **Required Documentation:** All support requests demanding billing refunds due to downtime must specify the date, verified duration of the outage, and the customer's account credentials.
4.  **Waiver Limits:** Support agents cannot authorize refunds exceeding the tier limits under any circumstances. Promises exceeding the cap violate corporate audit rules and will be flagged as compliant failures.
